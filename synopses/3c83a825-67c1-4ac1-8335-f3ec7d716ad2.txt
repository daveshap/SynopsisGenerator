In the year 2300, the planet Earth is a vastly different place than it is today. The major continents have shifted, the climate has changed, and new civilizations have arisen. In the Middle East, a new empire has taken hold. Theultanate of Araby is a powerful and prosperous empire that spans the entire region. However, all is not well in the land. In the shadows, a rebellion is brewing. A small group of rebels, calling themselves the "Free Araby" are determined to overthrow the Sultan and his government. The story begins with a group of children, mischievous by nature, who are playing in the streets of the capital city, Baghdad. They spy a man who they believe to be a member of the Free Araby. They follow him and eventually corner him in an alley. The man pleads with them not to turn him in, but the kids are not convinced. Just as they are about to turn him over to the authorities, they are interrupted by a bomb blast. The man uses the distraction to escape. The children are shaken by the explosion and quickly Scatter. As they make their way home, they notice that the city is in chaos. People are running and screaming, and the sound of gunfire can be heard in the distance. The children realize that the Free Araby has launched a full-scale rebellion. The story follows the children as they attempt to survive the uprising. They must dodge bullets and explosions, and find food and shelter. They eventually find themselves in the middle of the rebel stronghold. They meet the leaders of the Free Araby, and see firsthand the brutality of the Sultan's regime. The children are eventually captured by the Sultan's forces and taken to the palace. The Sultan is a cruel and sadistic man, and the children are horrified by the things they see and hear. The Sultan orders them to be executed, but they are saved at the last minute by the rebel leaders. They are freed and sent back to the rebel stronghold. The story culminates with the final battle between the rebel forces and the Sultan's army. The rebels are outnumbered and outgunned, but they are determined to win. The children play a pivotal role in the battle, using their knowledge of the city to help the rebels. In the end, the rebels are victorious and the Sultan is overthrown. The children are hailed as heroes and the new rulers of the land promise to build a better future for all.
